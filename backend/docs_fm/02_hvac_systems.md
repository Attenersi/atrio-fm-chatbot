# HVAC Systems — Heating, Ventilation & Air Conditioning

## System Overview

Meridian Business Center uses a central HVAC system managed by a Siemens Desigo CC building management system. The system includes:

- Central air handling units (AHUs) on floors 0, 2, and 4 (mechanical closets)
- Variable Refrigerant Flow (VRF) heat pump units for individual zone control
- Underfloor heating on floors 0 and 1 (lobby and ground floor offices)
- Individual thermostats in each office suite (Siemens RDG160 controllers)
- Fresh air ventilation with CO2-based demand control

## Temperature Control

Each office suite has its own thermostat located near the entrance door. Tenants can adjust the temperature within a range of 19°C to 24°C. The default setpoint is 21°C in winter and 23°C in summer.

The thermostat has a +/- 2°C adjustment range from the central setpoint. If a wider range is needed, contact facility management.

Common areas (lobby, hallways, kitchens) are maintained at 20°C in winter and 24°C in summer and cannot be adjusted by tenants.

## Heating Season

Heating is active from October 1 through April 30. Outside this period, heating is available on-demand if outdoor temperature drops below 14°C for more than 2 consecutive hours. The system switches automatically.

Underfloor heating on floors 0 and 1 operates on a slower response cycle — changes take 2–4 hours to take effect. Plan accordingly.

## Cooling Season

Active cooling is available from May 1 through September 30. The VRF system can provide cooling when outdoor temperature exceeds 22°C. Individual office units can be set to cooling mode via the thermostat.

Important: Do not open windows while the cooling system is active. This causes the system to overwork and can trigger a fault alarm. If you prefer fresh air, switch your thermostat to "off" before opening windows.

## Ventilation

Fresh air is supplied through the central AHUs and distributed via ceiling diffusers. The system adjusts airflow based on CO2 levels in each zone. During occupied hours (07:00–21:00), the ventilation runs in automatic mode. Outside occupied hours, the system switches to minimum ventilation.

Air filters are replaced every 6 months (March and September). During filter replacement, there may be a temporary reduction in airflow for approximately 30 minutes per floor.

## Common HVAC Issues and What to Do

**"My office is too cold"**
1. Check the thermostat — is it set to the desired temperature?
2. Check if windows are closed
3. Check if the thermostat display shows "heating" mode (flame icon)
4. If the thermostat is unresponsive, report it to facility management
5. Allow 30 minutes for the system to reach the set temperature

**"My office is too hot"**
1. Check the thermostat — lower the setpoint
2. Ensure no heat sources are blocking the thermostat sensor (printers, space heaters)
3. Check if windows are receiving direct sunlight — close blinds
4. If cooling doesn't activate, check if the season allows cooling (May–September)
5. Report persistent issues to facility management

**"There is a strange smell from the vents"**
This may indicate a dirty filter, moisture in the ductwork, or a blocked condensate drain. Report immediately to facility management. Do not attempt to open or modify any vent covers.

**"The thermostat is not responding"**
Thermostats are battery-powered (2x AA). If the display is blank or showing a low battery warning, contact facility management for battery replacement. Do not replace batteries yourself, as the thermostat needs recalibration after battery change.

**"There is water dripping from the ceiling unit"**
This indicates a blocked condensate drain in the VRF indoor unit. Report immediately — water damage can worsen quickly. Place a bucket underneath to catch water and move any electronics or documents away from the area.

## Scheduled Maintenance

- VRF system inspection: June (annual, by certified technician from Daikin)
- AHU belt and bearing inspection: November (annual)
- Ductwork cleaning: Every 3 years (next scheduled: October 2026)
- Legionella testing of humidification system: Quarterly

## Energy Saving Guidelines

- Set thermostat to 19°C when leaving the office for the day
- Close blinds on south-facing windows during summer afternoons
- Do not use personal space heaters — they overload the circuits and confuse the HVAC system
- Report any drafts from windows or doors — sealing issues waste energy
- Keep ventilation grilles unobstructed (no furniture, boxes, or papers over them)

## Emergency Procedures

In case of smoke or unusual fumes from the HVAC system:
1. Switch off the thermostat in your office
2. Leave the area immediately
3. Call the building emergency hotline: +31 (0)76 555 0199
4. Do not attempt to access mechanical rooms

# Electrical Systems — Power, Lighting & Outages

## Power Supply

Meridian Business Center is supplied by Enexis Netbeheer at 400V/230V three-phase. The main electrical room is located on the ground floor (Room G08, restricted access). Each floor has a sub-distribution panel in the electrical closet (locked, FM access only).

Each office suite has a dedicated electrical circuit providing:
- Standard wall outlets (230V, 16A Schuko type F): 6–8 per suite depending on size
- Dedicated data/power floor boxes: 2 per suite (near desk clusters)
- Maximum load per suite: 3.6 kW (16A circuit)

## What Tenants Can and Cannot Do

Allowed:
- Plug in standard office equipment (computers, monitors, desk lamps, phone chargers)
- Use power strips with surge protection (maximum 1 strip per outlet)
- Report any flickering lights, sparking outlets, or tripped breakers

Not allowed:
- Personal space heaters, fan heaters, or oil radiators (fire risk, circuit overload)
- Daisy-chaining power strips (connecting one strip to another)
- Accessing electrical panels or breaker boxes
- Plugging high-draw appliances (kettles, microwaves, toasters) into office outlets — use kitchen facilities only
- Running extension cords across walkways or under carpet

## Lighting

Office suites use LED panel lighting (Philips CoreLine, 4000K neutral white, dimmable). Lights are controlled by:
- Motion sensors in hallways, bathrooms, and common areas (automatic on/off)
- Manual switches in office suites (near entrance door)
- Daylight sensors in window-adjacent zones (auto-dim when natural light is sufficient)

Lighting is set to automatically switch off at 22:00. If you need lights after 22:00, use the manual override switch in your suite — lights will stay on for 2 hours before auto-off resumes.

The emergency lighting system (battery-backed) activates automatically during power failures and illuminates exit routes and stairwells for a minimum of 3 hours.

## Common Electrical Issues

**"The lights in my office won't turn on"**
1. Check the switch near the door — make sure it's in the "on" position
2. Check if other suites on the same floor have power (hallway lights on?)
3. If only your suite is affected, the breaker may have tripped — contact facility management
4. Do not attempt to reset breakers yourself

**"A power outlet is not working"**
1. Check if the device works in a different outlet
2. Check if a power strip is overloaded (red indicator light)
3. If multiple outlets are dead, a breaker likely tripped — contact facility management
4. If you see scorch marks or smell burning, evacuate immediately and call emergency line

**"Lights are flickering"**
Occasional flickering during storms is normal. Persistent flickering indicates a loose connection or failing driver. Report to facility management with the exact location (room number, which fixture).

**"Power outage in the entire building"**
Building-wide outages are rare (typically grid-related). During an outage:
1. Emergency lighting will activate automatically
2. Elevators will move to the ground floor and open doors
3. The server room (Room 411) has an uninterruptible power supply (UPS) providing 30 minutes of backup
4. Do not use candles or open flames
5. Wait for facility management announcements via email or building PA system
6. If the outage lasts more than 30 minutes, facility management will send an update email

## Solar Panels

The building has a 95 kWp solar panel installation on the roof (Floor 5). The solar panels contribute to the building's common area electricity consumption and reduce service charges. Individual tenants do not receive direct solar credits, but the savings are reflected in the annual service charge reconciliation.

## Electric Vehicle Charging

There are 8 EV charging stations in the parking garage (Level P1, spots P45–P52). Chargers are 22 kW Type 2 (AC). To use the chargers:
1. Plug in your vehicle
2. Tap your building access badge on the charger
3. Charging starts automatically
4. Cost: €0.30/kWh, billed monthly to your company's service charge account

Contact reception to register your badge for EV charging access. Registration takes 1 business day.

## Scheduled Maintenance

- Emergency lighting test: Monthly (first Monday, 09:00, 30-second test — lights will flash briefly)
- Full emergency lighting duration test: Annually in January (3-hour test on a Saturday)
- Electrical panel inspection: Annually in April (by certified electrician, Volker Wessels)
- Circuit breaker testing: Every 5 years (next: 2028)
- Solar panel cleaning and inspection: Twice per year (April and October)

## Emergency Procedures

In case of electrical fire or sparking:
1. Do not touch the source — do not attempt to unplug
2. If safe, switch off the breaker for that area (if you know which one and can access it safely)
3. Use a CO2 fire extinguisher (red with black label) — never use water on electrical fires
4. Evacuate the area
5. Call 112 and the building emergency hotline: +31 (0)76 555 0199

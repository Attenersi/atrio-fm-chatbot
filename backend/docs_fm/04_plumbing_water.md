# Plumbing & Water Systems

## Water Supply

The building is connected to Brabant Water municipal supply. Hot water for bathrooms and kitchens is provided by a central gas-fired boiler (Remeha Quinta Pro 115) located in the mechanical room (Floor 5). Hot water temperature is maintained at 60°C at the boiler and delivered at approximately 45°C at taps to prevent scalding.

## Bathrooms

Each floor has separate men's and women's bathrooms located near the elevator lobby. Ground floor also has an accessible/disabled bathroom near reception.

Bathroom facilities per floor:
- Men's: 3 toilets, 2 urinals, 2 sinks
- Women's: 4 toilets, 2 sinks
- Accessible (ground floor only): 1 toilet, 1 sink, grab bars, emergency pull cord

Bathrooms are cleaned twice daily (morning and afternoon) by the cleaning team. Soap, paper towels, and toilet paper are restocked daily. If supplies run out between restocks, contact reception.

## Kitchens

Each floor (1–4) has a shared kitchen with:
- Sink with hot and cold water
- Dishwasher (Bosch Serie 4, runs automatically at 18:00 daily)
- Boiling water tap (Quooker Fusion, provides instant 100°C water)
- Refrigerator (cleaned every Friday at 16:00 — remove personal items before then)
- Microwave oven
- Coffee machine (Jura X10, bean-to-cup)

The ground floor café/canteen has its own separate kitchen managed by the catering team.

Kitchen rules:
- Wash your own dishes or place them in the dishwasher
- Do not leave food in the refrigerator over the weekend — it will be discarded during Friday cleaning
- Do not pour cooking oil or food waste down the sink — use the waste bins
- Report any leaks, dripping taps, or drainage issues immediately
- The Quooker tap has a child safety lock — push and turn to activate boiling water

## Common Plumbing Issues

**"The toilet is clogged"**
Report to facility management immediately. Do not attempt to use chemical drain cleaners. Use the other bathrooms on the same floor or adjacent floor while the issue is resolved. A plunger is available in the cleaning supply closet on each floor (key at reception).

**"There is no hot water"**
1. Check if the issue affects only your floor or the whole building
2. Single floor: likely a local circulation pump issue — report to FM
3. Whole building: likely a boiler issue — FM is probably already aware, but report it
4. Hot water may take 30–60 seconds to reach taps on upper floors — let it run briefly

**"The tap is dripping"**
Report to facility management. A dripping tap wastes approximately 20 liters per day. Include the exact location (floor, room, which tap).

**"There is a water leak / flooding"**
1. If safe, try to identify the source and turn off the nearest shutoff valve (under sink or on the wall)
2. Move electronics and documents away from the water
3. Call facility management immediately: +31 (0)76 555 0101
4. If the leak is from a ceiling, evacuate the area below — water + electricity = danger

**"The kitchen drain is slow"**
Slow drains usually indicate a grease or food buildup. Report to FM. Do not use chemical drain cleaners — they damage the pipes.

**"The dishwasher is not working"**
1. Check if the door is fully closed (it won't start if ajar)
2. Check if the cycle is already running (display shows remaining time)
3. If the display shows an error code, note the code and report to FM
4. Do not attempt to reset or repair the dishwasher

## Water Shutoff Locations

In case of emergency, water shutoff valves are located at:
- Main building shutoff: Ground floor mechanical room G08 (FM access only)
- Per-floor shutoff: Utility closet on each floor (FM access only)
- Under-sink shutoffs: Accessible in kitchens and bathrooms (turn clockwise to close)

## Legionella Prevention

In compliance with Dutch law (Drinkwaterbesluit), the building's water system is tested quarterly for Legionella. The facility management team maintains a water safety plan (Waterveiligheidsplan). Hot water is stored at 60°C minimum and all low-use outlets are flushed weekly.

If you notice any taps or showers that have not been used in more than a week, please report this to facility management so they can be flushed.

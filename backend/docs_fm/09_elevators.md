# Elevators

## Elevator Overview

Meridian Business Center has 2 passenger elevators located in the central lobby:
- Elevator A (left): Serves floors 0–5, capacity 8 persons / 630 kg
- Elevator B (right): Serves floors 0–4 (does not go to floor 5), capacity 13 persons / 1000 kg

Elevator B is the larger unit and accommodates wheelchairs, furniture moves, and large deliveries. To use Elevator B for moving furniture or large items, book with reception 24 hours in advance — protective blankets will be installed.

Both elevators have:
- Automatic doors with sensor (hold door: press and hold the open button)
- Emergency phone (direct line to Securitas monitoring center)
- Emergency lighting (battery-backed)
- Alarm button (sounds alarm and contacts monitoring center)
- Mirror on rear wall

## Operating Hours

Elevators operate 24/7. During building hours (07:00–21:00), both elevators run in standard mode. After hours, both remain operational for badge-holding tenants.

## Common Elevator Issues

**"The elevator is stuck / I'm trapped"**
1. Stay calm — the elevator is safe and well-ventilated
2. Press the yellow alarm button (bell icon) — this connects directly to the Securitas monitoring center
3. The monitoring center will communicate with you through the elevator intercom
4. A certified technician will be dispatched (response time: typically 20–45 minutes)
5. Do NOT attempt to force doors open or climb out
6. If you have a mobile phone, you can also call the building emergency line: +31 (0)76 555 0199

**"The elevator doors won't close"**
1. Check if something is blocking the door sensor (bag, foot, small object on the floor)
2. Check if the "door open" button is stuck
3. If no obstruction is visible, use the other elevator and report the issue to reception

**"The elevator is making unusual noises"**
Report to facility management with details (which elevator, what kind of noise, when it happens). Some noise during acceleration and deceleration is normal.

**"The elevator skipped my floor"**
This happens during peak hours when many floors are called. Wait for the next cycle. If it consistently skips a floor, report to FM — the floor sensor may need calibration.

## Elevator Maintenance

Elevators are maintained by Kone Nederland B.V. under a full-service contract:
- Monthly inspection: Scheduled on the first Wednesday of each month (one elevator at a time, the other remains operational)
- Annual safety certification: Required by Dutch law (Warenwetbesluit liften), conducted in February
- Emergency service: Kone provides 24/7 emergency response for entrapments

During scheduled maintenance, the affected elevator displays an "Out of Service" sign. Use the other elevator. Maintenance typically takes 2–3 hours per elevator.

## Elevator Etiquette

- Let people exit before entering
- Hold the door for others approaching (press open button, don't block with hands)
- If the elevator is full, wait for the next one
- Do not jump or rock the elevator — this can trigger the safety brake
- Keep noise levels reasonable — sound carries through the shaft
- Report any spills, damage, or vandalism to reception

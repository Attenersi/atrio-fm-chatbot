# Cleaning & Waste Management

## Cleaning Schedule

Daily cleaning is performed by Hago Facility Solutions (Monday–Friday, 18:00–21:00):

Common areas (daily):
- Lobby and reception: vacuum, mop hard floors, wipe surfaces
- Hallways: vacuum, spot clean
- Bathrooms: full clean (disinfect toilets, sinks, mirrors, mop floors, restock supplies)
- Kitchens: clean countertops, sink, empty bins, wipe appliances, load dishwasher
- Elevators: wipe panels, mirror, mop floor
- Stairwells: sweep, mop landings

Office suites (daily unless tenant opts out):
- Vacuum floors
- Empty desk bins into central waste
- Wipe desks and surfaces (only if cleared — personal items are not moved)
- Clean glass partitions (weekly)

Special cleaning:
- Bathroom deep clean: Weekly (Saturday mornings)
- Kitchen deep clean: Weekly (Friday afternoons, 16:00)
- Window cleaning (exterior): Quarterly (March, June, September, December)
- Window cleaning (interior): Monthly
- Carpet deep clean: Twice per year (June and December, weekend schedule)
- Floor stripping and waxing (hard floors): Annually in August

## If You Need Extra Cleaning

For spills, accidents, or urgent cleaning needs during business hours, contact reception (+31 76 555 0100). The day-shift cleaner is available 08:00–12:00 for ad-hoc requests.

## Waste Separation

The Netherlands has strict waste separation requirements. The building provides the following bin types:

**In office suites:**
- Gray bin (under desk): Residual waste (restafval)
- Blue bin (near printer): Paper and cardboard (papier)

**In kitchens:**
- Green bin: Organic waste / food scraps (GFT)
- Blue bin: Paper and cardboard
- Yellow bin: Plastic packaging, drink cartons (PMD)
- Gray bin: Residual waste

**In hallways:**
- Paper (blue)
- Residual (gray)

**At loading dock (FM managed):**
- Large containers for: paper, residual, PMD, glass, electronic waste (e-waste)

## Waste Collection

External waste collection is handled by Saver (municipality contractor):
- Residual waste: Collected Tuesday and Friday
- Paper/cardboard: Collected Wednesday
- PMD (plastic, metal, drink cartons): Collected Thursday
- Glass: Collected monthly
- Organic: Collected Tuesday and Friday

## Tenant Responsibilities

- Separate your waste correctly — contamination leads to fines from the municipality
- Do not leave waste bags in hallways — use the bins provided
- Flatten cardboard boxes before placing in paper bins
- Do not dispose of electronics (keyboards, mice, chargers) in regular bins — bring to reception for e-waste collection
- Batteries: Collection box at reception desk
- Confidential documents: Shredder available in each floor's print room, or request a locked destruction bin from reception (€45/month)
- Large item disposal (furniture, equipment): Coordinate with FM — do not leave in hallways or loading dock without arrangement

## Sustainability Initiatives

Meridian Business Center holds a BREEAM-NL "Very Good" certification. Current sustainability measures:
- 95 kWp solar panels on roof
- LED lighting throughout (reduced energy by 40%)
- CO2-based ventilation demand control
- EV charging stations (8 spots)
- Rainwater collection for garden irrigation
- 100% green electricity contract (Garanties van Oorsprong certified)
- Annual energy audit as part of EED (Energie-Efficiëntie Directive) compliance

Tenants can contribute by:
- Separating waste correctly
- Turning off lights and equipment when leaving
- Using reusable cups and bottles (dishwasher available in kitchens)
- Reporting running taps, dripping toilets, or unnecessary lighting
- Using the stairs instead of elevators for 1–2 floor trips
